# Structural & Thermal Analysis

## 1. Structural Loads During 180° Inversion
Executing a 180-degree pitch inversion at 850 km/h (Mach 0.7) with 100% internal fuel volume generates extreme structural loads. 
* **Fuel Slosh Dynamics:** Because F1–F7 act as a continuous fuel spine, the AI must manage fuel transfer synchronously with the pitch maneuver to prevent center-of-gravity (CG) shifts from ripping the airframe apart. Fuel must be pumped toward the upper nodes (F1–F3) during the flip to counteract centrifugal forces.
* **Spine Tension:** The "Line Apex" acts as the primary load-bearing member. During the wide-arc inversion, the centrifugal force places the Apex Line under extreme tension. The airframe must be constructed from composite carbon-fiber reinforced polymers (CFRP) to survive the bending moment.
* **Fin Shear:** The static central Fin experiences massive shear stress when engines 1–6 vector 90° against it. The Fin must be structurally tied directly to the Apex spine, transferring the load through the fuselage rather than the wing surfaces.

## 2. Thermal Management (Thrust Stacking Heat)
Thrust stacking—aligning exhaust plumes from 6 engines in close proximity—creates a localized high-thermal zone at the rear of the aircraft.
* **Thermal Shielding:** The area immediately behind the Fin and engines 1–6 requires advanced thermal tiles or ablative coating. 
* **Fuel as Heat Sink:** The massive internal volume of Jet A fuel (3,200 kg) routed through nodes F1–F7 can be actively circulated along the inner hull to act as a heat sink, cooling the engine housings before the fuel is combusted.
